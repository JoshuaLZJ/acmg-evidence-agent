Literature

Key Publication (PMID:23269703 , 36927983)

Title: "Biallelic deleterious BRCA1 mutations in a woman with early-onset ovarian cancer" (2013) and "Clinical case study meets population cohort: identification of a BRCA1 pathogenic founder variant in Orcadians" (2023)

Key findings:

1. Functional evidence:

  • The variant p.Val1736Ala was identified in trans with a known pathogenic frameshift variant (c.2457delC; p.Asp821Ilefs*25) in a patient with biallelic BRCA1 mutations
  • The patient presented with:
  • Early-onset ovarian carcinoma at age 28
  • Short stature, microcephaly, developmental delay
  • Significant chemotherapy hypersensitivity
  • Tumors showed loss of wild-type BRCA1 (loss of heterozygosity)
  • BRCA1 p.Val1736Ala showed reduced DNA damage localization in functional assays

2. Segregation evidence:

  • The variant cosegregated with cancer in multiple families
  • Segregates with female breast and ovarian cancer in clinically ascertained Orcadian families

3. Founder variant:

  • Identified as a founder pathogenic variant in the Orcadian population
  • ~1% carrier frequency in Orcadians (20/2088)
  • Genealogical linkage to ancestors from the Isle of Westray, Orkney
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis supports population screening in this community

PubTator annotations:

  • Multiple associations confirmed between p.Val1736Ala (rs45553935) and ovarian cancer/hereditary breast and ovarian cancer syndrome
  • Positive correlation documented with drug hypersensitivity (chemotherapy toxicity)

Uncertainties

BP4
  • 1. MaveDB functional scores: No deep mutational scanning data available for this specific variant. However, this limitation is offset by the published functional studies demonstrating reduced DNA damage localization.
  • 2. SpliceAI interpretation: The minimal splice prediction is not unexpected for a missense variant but confirms the pathogenic mechanism is not splice-related. The BP4 criterion (low splice impact) does not diminish the strong evidence for pathogenicity through protein dysfunction.
  • 3. Molecular mechanism details: While the variant demonstrates impaired BRCA1 function (reduced DNA damage localization), the precise molecular mechanism at the protein structure level has not been fully characterized in published literature.
  • 4. Phenotype variability: The biallelic presentation in PMID:23269703 includes developmental features (microcephaly, short stature, developmental delay) that are not typical of monoallelic BRCA1 carriers. This suggests biallelic BRCA1 pathogenic variants may cause a distinct syndrome, though the monoallelic state clearly predisposes to breast/ovarian cancer based on segregation data.
  • 5. Population-specific considerations: The ~1% frequency in Orcadians is exceptionally high for a pathogenic variant. While this represents a founder effect and does not contradict pathogenicity (given the strong functional and clinical evidence), risk assessment and genetic counseling should account for this population-specific context.
