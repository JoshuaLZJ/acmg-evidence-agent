Population Genetics Evidence

Founder Variant Status (Retrieved from PMID:36927983 , 2023):

  • Identified as a founder variant in the Orcadian population (Northern Isles of Scotland, UK)
  • Specific origin traced to the Isle of Westray, Orkney
  • Allele Frequency in Orcadians: ~1% (20 out of 2,088 ORCADES research volunteers)
  • Relative Frequency: ~480-fold higher than in UK Biobank participants
  • Common haplotype observed around the variant, consistent with founder effect
  • Genealogical linkage established through birth, marriage, and death registrations

Segregation Evidence

Family Studies (Retrieved from PMID:36927983 & 23269703):

  • Variant segregates with female breast and ovarian cancer in multiple clinically ascertained families
  • Multiple families from Orkney with breast and ovarian cancer linked to this variant
  • Targeted testing in at-risk Orcadian families confirmed additional carriers

Functional Evidence

Molecular Studies (Retrieved from PMID:23269703 , 2013):

  • BRCA1 p.Val1736Ala showed reduced DNA damage localization in functional assays
  • Tumors associated with this variant showed loss of wild-type BRCA1 (loss of heterozygosity)
  • The variant co-segregated with cancer in multiple families

Biallelic Case Report:

  • First documented case of compound heterozygosity: one allele with c.5207T>C (p.Val1736Ala) and one allele with c.2457delC (p.Asp821Ilefs*25)
  • Patient presented with:
  • Early-onset ovarian carcinoma (age 28)
  • Short stature, microcephaly, developmental delay
  • Significant hypersensitivity to chemotherapy
  • This biallelic presentation supports pathogenicity of p.Val1736Ala
