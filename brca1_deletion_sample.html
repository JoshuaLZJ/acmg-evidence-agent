"Tissue-Specific Prevalence and Clonal Architecture of BRCA1/2 LOH-Inducing Chromosomal Aneuploidy"

Key Findings:

  • Chromosomal deletions involving 17q (BRCA1 locus) and 13q (BRCA2 locus) are consistently enriched in breast and ovarian cancers relative to other solid tumor types
  • Deletions, rather than point mutations, emerge as rate-limiting events in tumorigenesis
  • Mutational timing analysis places these deletions among the earliest somatic events in breast and ovarian cancers
  • In pre-malignant tissue from germline BRCA1/2 carriers, chr17q and chr13q deletions appear as localized subclonal events
  • In established malignancies, these deletions are found in dominant clonal lineages with widespread genomic instability
  • Breast and ovarian cellular environments confer a selective advantage for chr17q deletions
  • Statistical modeling indicates 1-3 deletions are sufficient to initiate tumorigenesis

Mechanistic Insights:

  • Deletion-associated chromosomal instability represents the central trigger for breast and ovarian cancer initiation
  • This explains the tissue-specific cancer risk in germline BRCA1/2 carriers
  • Somatic driver mutations in normal tissues may not predict malignant progression unless accompanied by deletions
